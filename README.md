# Stokes Phenomenon and Path Algebroid — Interactive Visualizer

[![Live Demo](https://img.shields.io/badge/demo-live-2ea44f)](https://dtq1997.github.io/Stokes-Matrix/)

An interactive visualizer for the Stokes matrices, central connection matrices,
and path-algebroid structure of the meromorphic connection

```math
\nabla \,=\, d \,-\, \Bigl(U + \frac{A}{z}\Bigr)\,dz
```

on the trivial rank $m$ bundle over $\mathbb{P}^{1}$, with a regular
singularity at $z=0$ and an irregular singularity of Poincaré rank one at
$z=\infty$.

The mathematics is organized around two background theories and two results
of the author's work:

- **(B1)** the asymptotic theory of linear difference equations, used here to
  produce *canonical analytic solutions* of an auxiliary *z*-shift system on
  the complex plane, with sharp control on the truncation of one-sided
  infinite matrix products;
- **(B2)** the Jimbo–Miwa–Ueno theory of isomonodromic deformation, under
  which the residue matrix $A$ evolves with $u=(u_{1},\dots,u_{n})$ so as to
  preserve all monodromy data;
- **(C1)** Tang–Xu's realization of every elementary Stokes matrix
  $S_{[\tau]}$ as a *one-sided infinite matrix product*, with factors coming
  from a representation of the Yangian $Y(\mathfrak{gl}_{n-1})$;
- **(C2)** Tang–Xu's *path-algebroid* description of the full Stokes data
  $\{S_{d}\}_{d\notin\mathrm{aS}(u)}$, in which every entry $(S_{d})_{ij}$ is
  the value of a representation $\rho_{U,A}$ on a directed path class with
  arguments, and the algebra structure is generated by a
  "right-detour minus left-detour" composition that explains the chamber /
  wall-crossing geometry as a sign-flip combinatorics.

## 1. The system and its monodromy data

Fix a *leading term*

```math
U \,=\, \mathrm{diag}\bigl(u_{1} I_{m_{1}},\,\dots,\,u_{n} I_{m_{n}}\bigr),\qquad u_{i}\in\mathbb{C},\ u_{i}\neq u_{j}\ (i\neq j),\ m_{i}\in\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1},
```

and a *residue matrix* $A\in\mathrm{Mat}_{m}(\mathbb{C})$, arbitrary, with

```math
m \,=\, m_{1}+\dots+m_{n}.
```

Here $n$ is the number of distinct eigenvalues of $U$ and $m$ is the size
of the system. Write $[A]$ for the block-diagonal part of $A$ adapted to
the spectral decomposition $\mathbb{C}^{m}=\bigoplus_{i=1}^{n}\mathbb{C}^{m_{i}}$
of $U$.

At $z=\infty$ the system $\nabla F=0$ has a unique formal fundamental
solution

```math
F^{[\infty]}(z)\,=\,\hat F(z)\,z^{[A]}\,e^{U z},\qquad \hat F(z)\,\in\,I+z^{-1}\mathrm{Mat}_{m}(\mathbb{C})[\![z^{-1}]\!],
```

generically divergent. The Balser–Jurkat–Lutz / Malgrange theorem asserts
that for every direction $d\in\mathbb{R}\setminus\mathrm{aS}(u)$ there is a
*unique canonical analytic fundamental solution* $F_{d}(z)$, holomorphic on
the sectorial region $\mathrm{Sect}_{d}$ of opening greater than $\pi$
around $\arg z=d$ and asymptotic there to $F^{[\infty]}(z)$. The set of
*anti-Stokes directions* is

```math
\mathrm{aS}(u)\,=\,\bigcup_{i\neq j}\bigl(-\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})+2\pi\mathbb{Z}\bigr)\,\subset\,\mathbb{R};
```

at $\tau=-\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})$, the exponential $e^{(u_{j}-u_{i})z}$ attains
its maximum rate of growth on the ray $\arg z=\tau$.

At $z=0$ the system has a regular singularity. When $A$ is non-resonant,
there is a unique convergent fundamental solution

```math
F^{[0]}(z)\,=\,H^{[0]}(z)\,z^{A},\qquad H^{[0]}(z)\,\in\,I+z\,\mathrm{Mat}_{m}(\mathbb{C})\{z\},\quad H^{[0]}(0)=I.
```

The **monodromy data** of $\nabla$ (cf. Tang–Xu, definition of monodromy
data) are the following matrix-valued invariants.

**Elementary Stokes matrix.** For each anti-Stokes direction
$\tau\in\mathrm{aS}(u)$,

```math
S_{[\tau]}(u,A) := F_{\tau+\varepsilon}(z)^{-1}\,F_{\tau-\varepsilon}(z), \qquad 0<\varepsilon\ll 1.
```

**Sectorial and normalized Stokes matrices.** For $d\notin\mathrm{aS}(u)$,

```math
S_{d}^{\pm}(u,A) := F_{d\pm\pi}(z)^{-1}\,F_{d}(z), \qquad S_{d}(u,A) := S_{d}^{+}(u,A) - S_{d}^{-}(u,A).
```

**Central connection matrix.**

```math
\Omega_{d}(u,A) := F_{d}(z)^{-1}\,F^{[0]}(z),
```

expressing the canonical solution at $z=\infty$ in the basis of the
canonical solution at $z=0$.

**Monodromy factor at $z=\infty$.** $\;e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}M_{d}(u,A)}$ with
$M_{d}=\Omega_{d}A\Omega_{d}^{-1}$.

These are related by

```math
e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}M_{d}}\,=\,(S_{d}^{-})^{-1}\,e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}[A]}\,S_{d}^{+},\qquad \Omega_{d\pm\pi}\,=\,S_{d}^{\pm}\,\Omega_{d}.
```

## 2. (B1) Asymptotic theory of linear difference equations

For each $k=1,\dots,n$, Tang–Xu attach to the system $\nabla F=0$ a
$(n-1)\times(n-1)$-block linear difference equation in a discrete variable
$\ell$:

```math
\Psi(\ell+1)\,=\,L_{k}(\ell)\,\Psi(\ell),\qquad L_{k}(\ell)\,=\,\frac{1}{u_{k}I-u_{\hat k\hat k}}\Bigl((\ell I+A_{\hat k\hat k})\,-\,A_{\hat k k}\,\frac{1}{\ell I+A_{kk}}\,A_{k\hat k}\Bigr),
```

where the hat removes the $k$th block row/column, $A_{kk}$ is the
$m_{k}\times m_{k}$ diagonal block of $A$, and so on. The matrix $L_{k}(\ell)$
— the recursive matrix $L_k$ of the system — is the algebraic
companion of $\nabla$ along the coalescence direction $k$.

The classical asymptotic theory of linear difference equations
(Birkhoff–Trjitzinsky, Braaksma; in the blocked case, the formulation used
in Tang–Xu) constructs from $L_{k}$ a pair of *canonical analytic
solutions* $\mathcal{L}_{k}^{+}(\ell)$ and $\mathcal{L}_{k}^{-}(\ell)$ on
half-planes in the discrete variable, given as one-sided convergent infinite
products:

```math
\mathcal{L}_{k}^{+}(1+\ell)^{-1}\,=\,(u_{k}I-u_{\hat k\hat k})^{1+\ell}\,\lim_{p\to\infty}\,p^{-\ell I-[L_{k}]_{ss}}\,\frac{(u_{k}I-u_{\hat k\hat k})^{p}}{p!}\,\overleftarrow{\prod_{r=1}^{p}}L_{k}(r+\ell),
```

and an analogous formula for $\mathcal{L}_{k}^{-}$ as $p\to-\infty$. These
limits exist under explicit non-resonance and dominance hypotheses, and the
tail of the product

```math
\Bigl|\Bigl(\overleftarrow{\prod_{r=K}^{p}}L_{k}(r+\ell)\Bigr)\,-\,\bigl(\text{leading asymptotic}\bigr)\Bigr|
```

is bounded uniformly in $p$ by a function of $K$ that can be made smaller
than any prescribed tolerance. This is the technical input that allows
truncating the infinite matrix products of Section 4 at finite order and
*certifying* the precision of the resulting Stokes-matrix entries —
including in the block case where some $m_{k}>1$, where the naïve
power-series resummation breaks down because the spectral gap closes within
a block.

## 3. (B2) Isomonodromic deformation

Vary $u=(u_{1},\dots,u_{n})$ in the regular configuration space

```math
(\mathbb{C}^{n})_{\mathrm{reg}}\,=\,\{u_{i}\neq u_{j}\}\,\subset\,\mathbb{C}^{n}.
```

By the theorem of Jimbo–Miwa–Ueno (1981), there is a unique flat connection
on the trivial $\mathrm{Mat}_{m}(\mathbb{C})$-bundle over
$(\mathbb{C}^{n})_{\mathrm{reg}}$ whose parallel transport preserves all
monodromy data $\{S_{[\tau]},\,\Omega_{d},\,e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}M_{d}}\}$. Its
horizontal sections solve the explicit nonlinear system, for $k=1,\dots,n$,

```math
\frac{\partial A}{\partial u_{k}}\,=\,\bigl[\,\mathrm{ad}_{u}^{-1}\mathrm{ad}_{\mathcal{E}_{k}}A,\,A\,\bigr],
```

where $\mathcal{E}_{k}=\mathrm{diag}(0,\dots,I_{m_{k}},\dots,0)$ is the
block projector for the $k$th eigenvalue and $\mathrm{ad}_{u}^{-1}$ acts on a block matrix
$X=(X_{ij})_{n\times n}$ by

```math
(\mathrm{ad}_{u}^{-1}X)_{ij}\,=\,\begin{cases}\dfrac{1}{u_{i}-u_{j}}X_{ij},&i\neq j,\\[4pt]0,&i=j.\end{cases}
```

The off-block-diagonal matrix $X_{k}:=\mathrm{ad}_{u}^{-1}\mathrm{ad}_{\mathcal{E}_{k}}A$
plays the role of the auxiliary connection of Schlesinger / Jimbo–Miwa–Ueno
type; the flow $\{\partial/\partial u_{k}\}$ is integrable, and the
solutions $A(u)$ have the strong Painlevé property (Miwa): they are
multi-valued meromorphic on $(\mathbb{C}^{n})_{\mathrm{reg}}$, with
branching only along the fat diagonal $\Delta=\{u_{i}=u_{j}\}$, and the
branching action on $A(u)$ is, under the Riemann–Hilbert–Birkhoff map, the
braid group action on the corresponding Stokes data. Within a single chamber of the
configuration space of $u$ the flow preserves every monodromy datum exactly;
crossing a real-codimension-one wall (a degeneration of some
$u_{j}-u_{i}$ to an anti-Stokes argument of the current direction $d$)
realizes a generator of the *isomonodromy braid action* on
$\{S_{[\tau]}\}$.

Operationally: dragging a puncture $u_{k}$ in the visualizer transports $A$
along the chosen path under this flow, so that the displayed $(S_{d},\Omega_{d})$
remain constant outside wall-crossings; the discontinuities a user sees
correspond exactly to anti-Stokes rays sweeping past $d$ during the motion.

## 4. (C1) Stokes matrices as one-sided infinite matrix products

Fix an anti-Stokes direction $\tau=-\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})$ and assume, in the
generic position, that no third puncture $u_{k}$ ($k\neq i,j$) lies on the
segment $[u_{i},u_{j}]$. The first main result of Tang–Xu expresses the
non-trivial $(i,j)$ block of the elementary Stokes matrix $S_{[\tau]}$ as a
limit of explicit one-sided products of $L_{j}$:

```math
(u_{j}-u_{i})^{A_{ii}}\,\frac{(S_{[\tau]})_{ij}}{2\pi\mathrm{i}}\,(u_{j}-u_{i})^{-A_{jj}}\,=\,\lim_{p\to\infty}\frac{(u_{j}-u_{i})^{p+1}}{p!}\,p^{[A_{ii}-A_{jj}-I]}\,\Bigl(\overleftarrow{\prod_{\ell=1}^{p}}L_{j}(\ell)\Bigr)_{i\hat{\jmath}}\,\Bigl(-\frac{1}{u_{j}I-u_{\hat{\jmath}\hat{\jmath}}}A_{\hat{\jmath}j}\Bigr),
```

with $\overleftarrow{\prod}_{m=1}^{p}L_{j}(m)=L_{j}(p)L_{j}(p-1)\cdots L_{j}(1)$
and $\hat{\jmath}$ the deletion of the $j$th block row/column. Tang–Xu
identify the truncated product $\overleftarrow{\prod}_{m=1}^{p}L_{j}(m)$ as
the evaluation, on a representation of the Yangian
$Y(\mathfrak{gl}_{n-1})$ pulled back from $U(\mathfrak{gl}_{n})$ along an
Olshanski-type homomorphism $O_{j}$, of an integer power of the universal
$\mathrm{T}$-operator. In the classical setting relevant to the
visualizer — where $A$ is a complex matrix rather than a matrix of Yangian
generators — the construction descends to the *r*-matrix Poisson structure
on $U(\mathfrak{gl}_{n})$ studied in Tang–Xu's JGP paper.

The truncation parameter $p$ is the **precision** exposed to the user:
$\mathcal{L}_{j}^{+}$ of Section 2 controls the tail uniformly, so $p$ may
be increased to drive the residual to any prescribed level, even when
$A$ has non-trivial Jordan structure on the diagonal blocks (the case
$m_{k}>1$).

A symmetric formula expresses $(S_{[\tau]})_{ij}$ as a one-sided product of
$L_{i}(\ell)$ with $\ell=-1,-2,\dots,-p$. The two presentations agree on the
overlap of their convergence regions, providing a built-in consistency
check used internally by the visualizer.

## 5. (C2) Path algebroid, representation, and wall-crossing

### 5.1 The path algebroid

To each configuration $u=(u_{1},\dots,u_{n})$ Tang–Xu attach a
$\mathbb{C}$-linear algebroid

```math
\mathfrak{S}(u_{1},\dots,u_{n},\infty)
```

with $n+1$ objects $u_{1},\dots,u_{n},u_{\infty}:=\infty$. For each pair
$(u_{s},u_{t})$ the hom-space $\mathrm{Hom}(u_{s},u_{t})$ is the
$\mathbb{C}$-linear span of $\delta_{st}$ and of all *path classes with
arguments* in $\mathbb{C}\setminus\{u_{1},\dots,u_{n}\}$ from $u_{s}$ to
$u_{t}$ (a path class with arguments is a homotopy class equipped with a
lift of the argument of the tangent direction at each endpoint).

The composition rule is *not* mere concatenation of paths. For path
classes $\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{sm}$, $\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{mt}$ meeting at
the intermediate object $u_{m}$, set

```math
\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{sm}\,\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{mt}\,:=\,\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{sm}\overset{r}{\circ}\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{mt}\,-\,\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{sm}\overset{l}{\circ}\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{mt},
```

where $\overset{r}{\circ}$ and $\overset{l}{\circ}$ are the
concatenations obtained by *deforming the joined path to the right* and *to
the left* of $u_{m}$ respectively. This **right-detour minus left-detour**
multiplication is the structural heart of the construction: each
factorization of a composite path picks up a $\pm$ sign for every
intermediate object, and the resulting sign sums are what convert geometric
data (which side of which puncture a path passes) into algebraic data
(linear combinations in $\mathrm{Hom}$).

A *representation* $(\mathcal{S},V_{1},\dots,V_{n})$ of $\mathfrak{S}$ is a
family of $\mathbb{C}$-vector spaces with $V_{\infty}=V_{1}\oplus\cdots\oplus V_{n}$
together with linear maps
$\mathcal{S}:\mathrm{Hom}(u_{s},u_{t})\to\mathrm{Hom}(V_{s},V_{t})$
respecting the right-minus-left multiplication.

### 5.2 The representation associated with $(U,A)$

Tang–Xu construct a canonical representation $\rho_{U,A}$ with
$V_{i}=\mathbb{C}^{m_{i}}$, characterized by

```math
(S_{d})_{ij}\,=\,\rho_{U,A}\bigl(\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)}\bigr),\qquad i\neq j,\ d\notin\mathrm{aS}(u),
```

where $\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)}\in\mathrm{Hom}(u_{i},u_{j})$ is the
*Stokes path* assigned to the pair $(i,j)$ in direction $d$: concretely,
the homotopy class of the straight segment from $u_{i}$ to $u_{j}$, with
endpoint arguments determined by the lift of
$\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})$ closest to $-d$, and with the prescribed crossing
pattern at the intermediate cuts emanating from each
$u_{k}$ ($k\neq i,j$) in direction $-d$.

On the elementary generators provided by Section 4
($\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{\mathrm{straight}}$, the bare straight segment
without intermediate punctures on it), $\rho_{U,A}$ is the value of the
one-sided infinite product. The right-minus-left multiplication then
*propagates* this value to every other path class:

```math
\rho\bigl([i]\,\alpha\,[m]\bigr)\,\cdot\,\rho\bigl([m]\,\beta\,[j]\bigr)\,=\,\rho\bigl([i]\,\alpha{-}\beta\,[j]\bigr)\,-\,\rho\bigl([i]\,\alpha{+}\beta\,[j]\bigr),
```

where $[i]\,\alpha\,[m]$ denotes a path from $u_{i}$ to $u_{m}$ together
with the sign string $\alpha\in\{+,-\}^{\bullet}$ recording, in order, which
side of each intermediate puncture the path passes, and $\alpha\pm\beta$ is
the concatenated sign string with the join sign flipped between $+$ and
$-$.

### 5.3 Chamber and wall-crossing geometry

The complement $\mathbb{R}\setminus\mathrm{aS}(u)$ is partitioned into
**chambers**; on a chamber, every path class
$\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)}$ is locally constant, so $S_{d}$ is too.
Adjacent chambers differ by an anti-Stokes ray, and the corresponding
elementary Stokes matrix $S_{[\tau]}$ effects the transition: from
Section 1,

```math
S_{d}^{\pm}\,=\,\prod_{\tau\,\in\,\mathrm{aS}(u)\cap (d,d\pm\pi)} S_{[\tau]}\quad(\text{ordered along the half-circle}),\qquad S_{d}\,=\,S_{d}^{+}-S_{d}^{-}.
```

In the path-algebroid picture this is exactly the propagation of $\rho_{U,A}$
over the **sign hypercube** of chamber labels: for fixed $(i,j)$ with
$L=\lvert\text{label}(u_{i})-\text{label}(u_{j})\rvert$, the
$2^{L-1}$ sign patterns $\alpha\in\{+,-\}^{L-1}$ form the vertices of a
hypercube, the recursive relation above is one edge of the hypercube
(flipping one sign), and a single anti-Stokes ray sweeping past $d$
corresponds to flipping one bit of $\alpha$. The full $S_{d}$ at any
chamber is obtained from the elementary anchors (the straight Stokes paths
without intermediate punctures, evaluated by Section 4) by traversing the
hypercube once.

Concretely, the entry of the normalized Stokes matrix at a reference
chamber $d_{\mathrm{reg}}$ has the all-minus form

```math
(S_{d_{\mathrm{reg}}})_{kt}\,=\,\rho_{U,A}\bigl([\text{label}(u_{k})]\,\underbrace{{-}{-}\cdots{-}}_{L-1}\,[\text{label}(u_{t})]\bigr),
```

and crossing a single anti-Stokes ray $\tau=-\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})$ between two
chambers (swapping the adjacent labels $i,j$ in the angular order) sends a
matrix $S$ on one side to a matrix $S'$ on the other given block-wise by

```math
S'_{ij}=S_{ij},\quad S'_{ji}=e^{-2\pi\mathrm{i}A_{jj}}\,S_{ji}\,e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}A_{ii}},\quad S'_{kj}=S_{kj}-S_{ki}S_{ij}\ (k\neq i,j),
```

with all other blocks unchanged. Cycling $d\mapsto d+2\pi$ composes to the
formal monodromy $e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}[A]}$.

## 6. What the visualizer shows

- **Left panel.** The configuration data: $n$ and the multiplicities
  $m_{1},\dots,m_{n}$; the eigenvalues $u_{1},\dots,u_{n}$; the residue
  matrix $A$ of size $m\times m$; the direction $d$ (in units of $\pi$);
  and a precision selector controlling the truncation order $p$ of the
  one-sided infinite product of Section 4.
- **Middle panel.** The complex $u$ plane. Punctures $\{u_{k}\}$ are
  draggable; anti-Stokes cuts emanate from each puncture in direction
  $-d$ (dashed); the chamber containing $d$ is shaded; and the Stokes
  path $\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)}$ of the selected entry is drawn,
  with its waypoints draggable within the homotopy class. The sign string
  $\alpha\in\{+,-\}^{L-1}$ of the path is the topological invariant being
  visualized.
- **Right panel.** The normalized Stokes matrix $S_{d}$ and the central
  connection matrix $\Omega_{d}$ as $m\times m$ block
  grids, with several view modes (block, full numerical, polar). Clicking
  an $(i,j)$ block selects the entry; the selected-entry display shows the
  value of $\rho_{U,A}(\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)})$ to the configured
  precision.
- **Direction slider.** The direction $d$ ranges continuously over an
  interval of length $2\pi$; dragging rotates the cuts, updates the
  chamber, and propagates $S_{d}$ by the wall-crossing rule of
  Section 5.3. Anti-Stokes rays and chamber centers are marked on the
  slider track.

Dragging a puncture realizes the isomonodromy flow of Section 3 along the
chosen path of $u$: $A$ is updated so $(S_{d},\Omega_{d})$ remain constant outside
wall-crossings, and any visible discontinuity is an anti-Stokes ray
sweeping past $d$. Dragging a path vertex changes the geometric
representative of $\boldsymbol{\gamma}_{ij}^{(d)}$ within its homotopy
class but not its sign string $\alpha$, illustrating the
representation-theoretic invariance of the Stokes entry.

## 7. Conventions

- $m=\sum_{i}m_{i}$; $n$ is the number of distinct eigenvalues of $U$;
  $[A]$ is the block-diagonal part of $A$ adapted to $U$.
- Imaginary unit: $\mathrm{i}$.
- Anti-Stokes set: $\mathrm{aS}(u)=\bigcup_{i\neq j}(-\arg(u_{j}-u_{i})+2\pi\mathbb{Z})$.
- Stokes-matrix conventions: $S_{d}^{\pm}=F_{d\pm\pi}^{-1}F_{d}$,
  $S_{d}=S_{d}^{+}-S_{d}^{-}$. The formal monodromy at $z=\infty$ is
  $e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}[A]}$, satisfying $e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}M_{d}}=(S_{d}^{-})^{-1}e^{2\pi\mathrm{i}[A]}S_{d}^{+}$.
- Central connection matrix $\Omega_{d}=F_{d}^{-1}F^{[0]}$.
- Order of multiplication in infinite products:
  $\overleftarrow{\prod}_{\ell=1}^{p}L_{j}(\ell)=L_{j}(p)\cdots L_{j}(1)$.
- $[i]\,\alpha\,[j]$ denotes a path class from $u_{i}$ to $u_{j}$ with
  intermediate sign string $\alpha\in\{+,-\}^{\bullet}$ recording which
  side of each intermediate puncture the path passes; $\alpha\pm\beta$
  denotes the concatenation with the join sign set to $\pm$.
